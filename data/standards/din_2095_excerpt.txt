DIN 2095 (simplified excerpt) — Cylindrical helical compression springs made
from round wire — Quality requirements for dimensions and calculation.

Clause 4.1 — Spring index. The spring index C, defined as the ratio of mean
coil diameter D to wire diameter d (C = D/d), shall be between 4 and 20 for
compression springs manufactured from round wire. Values below 4 make
coiling difficult and induce excessive stress concentration at the wire
surface. Values above 20 make the spring prone to tangling and buckling.

Clause 5.3 — Slenderness and buckling. The slenderness ratio, defined as the
free length L0 divided by the mean coil diameter D, shall not exceed 5.26 for
springs with fixed-free end conditions (one end free to rotate and translate)
to avoid lateral buckling under axial load. Springs guided within a sleeve or
over a mandrel may tolerate a higher slenderness ratio; consult the guided
end-condition tables in Annex B.

Clause 6.1 — Allowable shear stress. The corrected shear stress, computed by
applying the Wahl stress-concentration factor to the nominal torsional shear
stress, must not exceed 45 percent of the wire's tensile yield strength Sy
for static (non-cyclic) loading conditions, and must not exceed 30 percent of
Sy for dynamic (fatigue, cyclic) loading conditions. Exceeding these limits
indicates an unacceptable risk of yielding or fatigue failure within the
specified service life.

Clause 6.4 — Fatigue safety factor. For springs subjected to cyclic loading,
a fatigue safety factor of at least 1.3, computed against the Goodman
fatigue criterion, is required before the design may be approved for
production. Designs below this threshold shall be flagged for redesign,
typically by reducing wire stress or selecting a higher-fatigue-strength
alloy.

Clause 7.2 — Heat treatment. Compression springs manufactured from
cold-drawn or oil-tempered wire shall be stress-relieved after coiling at a
temperature and duration appropriate to the wire grade, in accordance with
ASTM A125, to reduce residual coiling stresses before shot peening or final
inspection.
