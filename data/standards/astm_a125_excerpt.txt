ASTM A125 (simplified excerpt) — Standard Specification for Steel Springs,
Helical, Heat-Treated.

General requirements. Heat-treated helical compression and extension springs
shall be stress-relieved at a minimum temperature of 200 degrees Celsius for
not less than 20 minutes after coiling, in order to reduce residual stresses
introduced during the coiling operation and to stabilize the free length of
the spring prior to final inspection.

Shot peening. Springs intended for cyclic (fatigue) service should be shot
peened after stress relief to induce beneficial residual compressive
stresses at the wire surface, which measurably improves fatigue life
compared to an equivalent un-peened spring of the same material and
geometry.

Surface finish. The wire surface shall be free of seams, laps, and other
defects that could act as fatigue crack initiation sites. Visual and
dye-penetrant inspection is recommended for springs used in critical
cyclic-load applications.
